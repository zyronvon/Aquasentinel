Project info & pipeline

AquaSentinel uses a USB camera to capture water sample images, runs a local CNN model, and surfaces the resulting predictions and diagnostics through a lightweight dashboard.

Our mission

AquaSentinel is a research-driven project dedicated to detecting microplastics in water samples using advanced AI technology. Built with state-of-the-art CNN (Convolutional Neural Network) models and open-source tools, our platform contributes towards cleaner oceans and a sustainable future for marine ecosystems.

We believe that by combining cutting-edge artificial intelligence with environmental science, we can provide researchers, scientists, and conservationists with the tools they need to understand and combat plastic pollution in our oceans.

AI-powered detection

Our CNN models are trained on microplastics datasets to identify and classify particles in water samples with high confidence.

Comprehensive analysis

Confidence histograms, sample predictions, and summary statistics support downstream research and reporting.

Research-focused

Built for students and researchers who need quick insight into model behaviour rather than a full product.

Open science

Code and outputs are open so others can reproduce, audit, and extend the work for their own projects.

Technology & methodology

Deep learning

CNN models for microplastics image classification and confidence estimation.

Python & TensorFlow

Local model training and inference executed on the operator machine.

Computer vision

Pre-processing of USB camera frames and region-of-interest extraction.

Data analytics

Aggregation of predictions into histograms, summaries, and exportable CSVs.

Impact goals

  • Enable faster and more accurate microplastic detection
  • Support marine research and conservation efforts
  • Contribute to global ocean health monitoring
  • Advance AI applications in environmental science
  • Facilitate data-driven policy decisions

Future developments

  • Real-time analysis capabilities
  • Mobile app for field researchers
  • Integration with IoT sensors

  • Global database collaboration
